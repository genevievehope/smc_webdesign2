Genevieve Johnson

graphic designer, UX researcher, artist

Hello, thanks for your interest! I’m a Los Angeles-based graphic design student concentrating in user experience at Santa Monica College. I’m determined to use my passion for graphic design and intuitive interactions to leave the world better than I found it.

I mainly stick to pencil, paper, paint, and the Adobe Creative Suite (Illustrator, InDesign, and Photoshop) for design work, and I branch out to a few other tools (Invision and Balsamiq, among others) for user testing. Transparency is key – I make an effort to clearly communicate messages in my work through bold typography and thoughtful colors. I’m particularly proud of work from the summer of 2018, when I interned as a graphic designer at a nonprofit called 826LA. I created book covers, a wholesale catalog, email banners, and posters to help programmers instill kids with writing skills and confidence.

Although most of my experience is in graphic design, I’ve conducted academic user research projects as well. Learning about people’s design opinions never fails to fascinate me.
